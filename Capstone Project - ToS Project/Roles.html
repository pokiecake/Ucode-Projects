Factions

There are 2 main factions and another faction lurking around. The 2 main factions are the town and the mafia. The last faction is the neutral faction who have unique goals to win. The town has to lynch every evildoer, who are the mafia and some of the Neutrals. The mafia has to kill anyone who does not submit to the mafia, who are the town and some of the Neutrals. The Neutrals have different goals and abilities than each other. Some of them have to kill the town and/or mafia, while some have to survive or have weirder goals. When some neutrals win their goals, the game doesn't need to end, but when the town, mafia, or certain neutrals win the game, the game ends since they killed all of the main factions. All roles will fit into a faction.

Alignments

All roles have an alignment, which aren't super important to the game. The Alignment summarizes what your role is about. The current alignments are Investigative, protective, killing, support, deception, evil, benign, and Chaos. The town alignments are the first 4 alignments. Alignments go after your faction, so it would be Town Investigative, Town Protective, and so on. The mafia alignments are the 3rd, 4th, and 5th alignment. You won't naturally find someone saying those alignments on Town of salem. The Neutral alignments are the last 3 alignments and killing. The Neutrals have unique goals depending on their alignment. The benign alignment don't necessarily have to kill to win. The Neutral evil alignment have three roles, the witch, jester, and executioner. They have unique goals to the Neutral faction. The Neutral killing faction have to kill the Town, mafia, and neutrals against their goal. They are usually the lone wolf, but they can win with the same role. The Neutral chaos alignment cause chaos to the town with unique abilities. These are the alignments in ToS.

Role List

When you start a game, there is a role list. The role list tells you which roles and/or alignments that are in the game. All of the roles take one spot on the role list. All game modes have fixed game modes, except custom, which allows the host, the first player to join the lobby, to make the role list. If enough people in the lobby types /repick, the host is randomly picked. The role list allows people to claim a fake role and for the evils to strategize their kills. The roles also allow the Town to pick off roles when people are confirmed town or once people die, making it harder for evils to claim.

The Investigator

The investigator has the ability to find at least 3 possible roles that a person can be. The investigation results are fixed, but they can be changed by a framer, hex master, or arsonist. This makes it hard to fake-claim if an investigator is in the game. You shouldn't be too afraid of investigators since there is a chance that there might not be one in game modes other than custom and classic. You need to think of roles to fake-claim in case someone asks you to claim a role, or your role won't be possible with the role list.

Keywords

  • Rampage - Anyone who visits the house you rampaged will be dealt the attack value the role has.
  • Attack and Defense - Attack and defense are on a spectrum. If the attack is lower on the same place on the spectrum as the target's defense, they will live. Otherwise, they will die. Lynching will always kill someone.
  • Investigation results - Investigation results will show 3 or more possible roles their target can be. Anyone's investigation results will be whatever role they are, with some exceptions. If a disguiser disguises as someone, the role they disguised as will be their investigation results. If the investigator's target is doused or hexed, their results will be in the Arsonist's and hex master's results respectively. Finally, a juggernaut has no investigation result.

    • Bodyguard, Godfather, Arsonist, Crusader
    • Investigator, Consigliere, Mayor, Tracker, Plaguebearer
    • Vigilante, Veteran, Mafioso, Pirate, or Ambusher
    • Lookout, Forger, Witch/Coven Leader
    • Sheriff, Executioner, Werewolf, or Poisoner
    • Medium, Janitor, Retributionist, Necromancer, or Trapper
    • Spy, Blackmailer, Jailor, or Guardian Angel
    • Escort, Consort, Transporter, Hypnotist
    • Survivor, Vampire hunter, Amnesiac, Medusa, or Psychic
    • Framer, Jester, Vampire, or Hex Master
  • Douse - Dousing someone will mean that there is gasoline on them. They will not know that they are doused (unless they are an Arsonist). Only the arsonist can douse, and if the arsonist ignites, everyone who is doused dies (unless they have invinible defense as an arsonist's ignite has an unstoppable attack). Dousing will also change their results to Bodyguard, Godfather, Arsonist, and Crusader. Gasoline cannot be removed unless an Arsonist does nothing during the night and clean the gas off themselves. Gasoline can also be removed by a Guardian Angel if they watch over their target.
  • Hex - Hexing someone will change their results to framer, jester, vampire, hex master. They will also appear as a member of the Coven. No one will know that they are hexed. The hex can be removed by a Guardian Angel.
  • Astral - This means that the role won't visit the target, but still do their ability on them. This means a lookout can't see that target visiting the player, and a tracker can't track them visitng someone. Their astral visit can't be intercepted by a bodyguard, veteran, trapper, werewolf, or pestilence.
  • Full moon nights - Full moon nights are on even nights, while odd nights are non-full moon nights. Even nights are also when the werewolf transforms.

General Footnotes

The Forger, Werewolf, Vampire, Hypnotist, and Necromancer were roles suggested by the community.
The victory screen that shows up at the end depend on how big the faction is. This means that a Survivor win screen would only occur when only the survivor wins because of a draw. A pirate wins screen is when only pirate wins, etc.

Town Footnotes

If a vampire hunter turns into a vigilante, and an Amnesiac remembers as vampire, the vigilante will not turn into a vampire.
A bodyguard will kill anyone who harms their target, also sacrificing their life to do so. This means that if a vampire hunter visits a vampire, and a bodyguard guards the vampire, the vampire hunter and bodyguard will die as the vampire hunter intended to harm the bodyguard's target. It will be obvious that the person the vampire hunter visited is vampire, as that is the only way this situation can happen.
If a psychic survives when there are 2 other players alive, and it is an odd night, they will receive the vision,"The Town is too small to accurately find an evildoer." If the psychic is alive when no more Town or Neutral Benign is alive, and it is a full moon night, they will receive the vision, "The town is too evil to find anyone good!"

Mafia Footnotes

If there are no mafioso or godfather in the mafia, a random mafia member will be promoted to a mafioso.
When the disguiser dies, their name and role will still be in the mafia chat. This is to make sure that the Mafia members don't forget that the disguiser has disguised as another role.

Neutral Footnotes

Not all neutrals will work together to win, especially if it is between two neutral Killings.
When Amnesiac remembers a role and wins, it is counted as an Amnesiac win.
It is possible for a plaguebearer victory to occur. This happens when everyone is wiped out and the plaguebearer hasn't turned into pestilence. It could also occur when it is a plaguebearer with roles that win with Plaguebearer (survivor, Amnesiac, pirate, etc.) and the Plaguebearer hasn't infected them yet.

Coven Keywords

Necronomicon - On night 3, the Coven will get the Necronomicon. The Coven leader will be the first one to get the necronomicon if they are alive. If the person holding the necronomicon dies, it goes to the person who join the lobby earlier. The necronomicon gives the role a buff to whoever holds it, as well as give that person detection immunity.
